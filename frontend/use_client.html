6. Safety, Identity, and Ethics

First-Person Mirror & Voice Crystal

Echo's ethics are baked into its architecture. The system is designed to be a first-person mirror, not a chatbot persona. Every echoed phrase is in the user's own cloned voice and first-person perspective, preventing confusion with therapist or assistant voices.

  • Clone-or-silence rule: If identity congruence cannot be maintained, the system prefers silence over a generic synthetic voice.
  • GCL gating: When coherence drops below a clinician-defined threshold, the Deep Reasoning Core is locked out; only simple mirroring is allowed.
  • Offline by design: All audio processing, modeling, and logging occur on-device. No cloud APIs listen to the home environment.
